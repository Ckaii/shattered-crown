The World

A Kingdom Built on a Lie

The war is over. The wrong side won.

The Premise

The legendary hero Aldric slew the Demon Lord and seized the throne. The world believed it had found a saviour. What it got was another tyrant.

"He didn't come home. Weeks later, the king was dead. Aldric claimed divine mandate and took the crown. Most cheered. A few quietly disappeared."

You are Varn — a debt-ridden border manor lord keeping your estate alive through mercenary contracts. In a crumbling fortress, your crew unearthed the Demon Lord's Sword.

You touched it. Vision fragments poured in — Aldric's face at the Demon Lord's final moment, a voice that sounded like two people speaking at once, eyes too cold for someone who just saved the world.

The sword remembers everything. And Aldric knows it's been found.

♛ Varn — The Debtor

You never asked to be a hero. You're a manor lord with creditors, managing a rotating cast of expendable mercenaries. The sword chose you anyway.

⚔ Aldric — The King

The world's celebrated champion sits the throne. He is actively hunting the sword — and everyone who has touched it. His response escalates with every truth uncovered.

🗡 The Demon Lord's Sword

Not a weapon. A witness. It absorbed the Demon Lord's final moments — and has been waiting to tell someone. Each soul that feeds it unlocks another fragment of the real story.

How It Works

Systems Built Around Loss

Death isn't failure. Death is fuel.

🎲
d20 Tactical Combat

Baldur's Gate-style grid combat with Action & Bonus Action economy. Positioning matters — flanking grants advantage, high ground extends range, environmental hazards reward creativity over brute force.

DnD 5e Inspired
💀
Permanent Death

Every adventurer who falls is gone. Permanently. You don't restart — you grieve, regroup, and hire from the next tavern. Losing a roster isn't game over. It's a consequence.

Roguelike Core
🗡
Soul Absorption

Each fallen adventurer's soul feeds the Demon Lord's Sword. At thresholds of 3, 7, 12, and 20 souls, the sword awakens — unlocking vision sequences that reveal the conspiracy piece by piece.

Story Progression
🤝
Trust System

Mixed human-monster rosters unlock unique combined abilities — but start with Trust penalties. Shared victories build trust; betrayals fracture it. The political divide lives in your party mechanics.

The Core Loop

Hire · Fight · Lose · Learn

Every run is a chapter. Every death moves the story forward.

1
🍺
Hire
Recruit from the local tavern. Humans, elves, former monster-soldiers — each with permanent stats and personalities.
2
🗺
Choose Path
Navigate a branching node map. Combat encounters, random events, rest points — risk versus resource management.
3
⚔
Fight
Tactical grid combat. Flank, use terrain, manage action economy, unlock joint moves if trust is high enough.
4
💀
Lose Someone
Permanent death. Their soul feeds the sword. Grief is a mechanic, not a screen wipe.
5
🔮
Vision Unlocks
Hit a soul threshold — the sword shows another piece of the truth. Aldric responds. Pressure escalates.
6
↺
Adapt
Hire again with fewer options. New bounties are posted. The king's hunters are named now. What do you do?
